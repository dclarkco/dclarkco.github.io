For this website, I took inspiration from a number of websites, ultimately settling on the iPortfolio template, with a heavy slew of modifications.

Kuon Yagi's eccentric design language inspired me, I especially like how his site has a nice mix of fonts, dark color schemes, and a scrolling home page with all the information broken into scrollable sections. Likewise, his skills page gives a nice visual breakdown of his abilities. Another plus is the use of whitespace to emphasize certain information.

Daniel Polevoy has a really nice profile page with some minimalist text effects and a scroll-down style experience page. It is a bit text heavy, but the sheer volume of text emphasizes his experience and I like that the page just keeps going. There is a vast collection of expertise under his belt, effectively communicated by the home page's sleek and content-forward design.

Eugene So uses a format that breaks the main page into halves, text on left, image on the right, with a scrolling feed of works. It's a nice example of the aesthetic and dark/colorful design I like alot. Once I have more portfolio items to show, I'll be implementing them like Eugene has.
