The Office enjoyed a period of popularity that few sit coms ever see. Even post-mortem, the show is a cult classic with some of the highest sustained review scores for sit coms, especially. 10 weeks ago, I argued this was due to the unique talent and creative diversity in the writers room, ultimately carried by a writers room that had the unique position of being part of the key character actors. The Office is one of the greatest shows of all time for this, and a multitude of reasons.

Were I to pitch this 'research' to a company like Netflix, for the purposes of getting a "The Office" film approved for production, I'd create a deck of reveal.js slides with some key visualizations. First of all, I'd present a slide showing The Office viewership over time, as it enjoyed a few seasons of consistent live viewership. Likewise, showing some recent viewership data would be helpful, as the show has been exceptionally popular as of late. Second, I'd show revenue both during production and post-mortem, as well as a projection of how much a new film might earn. Last, maybe I'd compare film reboots of other hit shows or movies, for contextual purposes.

Else, if I were to pitch the same thing to a design studio, in charge of producing new art and posters for a "The Office" film, I would take a different approach. For these purposes, I think a mixed metric of ratings & viewership to identify the primo fan-favorite episodes and then pick out key scenes. Perhaps then, I'd make a short video, or set of infographics to present alongside this information because I can emphasize graphical styles or particular screen-grabs easier.

Last, to present this information to fans I'd choose a few social media sites, probably Twitter, Reddit, and TikTok, for their reach and creative possibilities. I'd post some infographics, made using altair or tableau, and touched up with illustrator. This way, fans would have a bit of insider perspective into the history of the show, and why a reboot will be epic. I'd also disseminate some of the posters mocked up earlier, to try and stir up public interest in the show.
